#### Overall Impression

The design is moving in the right direction. It now has a dominant central object and the pairwise choice finally feels like the hero of the interface. The biggest opportunity is to stop designing the gallery wrapper and start designing the user's moment: "pick one, keep moving."

#### What's Working

The single workspace surface is much stronger than the previous multi-card layout. It gives the page one place where the task happens.

The two large choice targets are the best product idea here. They communicate the core interaction faster than any explanatory text could.

The OKLCH palettes are deliberate, especially Paper Desk. It avoids generic dark SaaS and gives the tool a warmer personality without making the controls strange.

#### Priority Issues

**[P1] Gallery Frame Is Louder Than The Workflow**

**Why it matters**: Users are trying to compare items, not evaluate "Paper Desk" as a concept. The rail, mood line, and scene sentence make the surface feel internal-facing.

**Fix**: Demote variant metadata into a compact selector or remove it from the eventual product UI. Use task-based copy such as "Choose the better option" and show the list name or remaining comparisons instead.

**Suggested command**: `distill`

**[P1] Mobile Breaks The Pairwise Moment**

**Why it matters**: Pairwise comparison works best when both options are visible together. On a 390px viewport, the second option is low enough that the first screen requires visual recovery.

**Fix**: On mobile, compress the top strip, move export below completion, reduce option height, and keep both choices plus VS within the first viewport.

**Suggested command**: `adapt`

**[P2] Secondary Tools Compete With The Decision**

**Why it matters**: Add items, queue, ranking, and export are useful, but they are not all relevant during every vote. Showing them during the choice increases extraneous load.

**Fix**: Keep current pair and progress primary. Put queue/ranking behind tabs, a lower drawer, or a post-choice/post-completion state. Hide export until completion.

**Suggested command**: `layout`

**[P2] Action Feedback Is Underdesigned**

**Why it matters**: The core loop needs a satisfying beat after each choice. Without selected, next, undo, or confirmation states, the preview feels static.

**Fix**: Add selected state, a brief confirmation, next-pair transition, and "Undo last choice." Represent disabled/loading states for Add Items and Export.

**Suggested command**: `animate`

**[P3] Copy Is Concept-Rich But Task-Poor**

**Why it matters**: "Warm, tactile, low friction" helps evaluate a design direction, but it does not help someone complete a ranking.

**Fix**: Replace mood copy with operational copy in the product view. Keep concept copy only in the gallery controls or an internal note.

**Suggested command**: `clarify`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User)**: Alex has no visible shortcut model for choosing left/right, undoing, skipping, or exporting. A repeated pairwise workflow needs keyboard accelerators or it will feel slow.

**Jordan (First-Timer)**: Jordan can see two large choices, but the page never plainly says "choose one." The labels "Ranking drafts," "Paper Desk," and "Queue" add interpretation work before the first action.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: Casey sees progress and export before completing the comparison, while the second choice sits lower in the first mobile viewport. That weakens the one-handed "tap the better option" flow.

#### Minor Observations

The mobile tabs are accessible by ARIA label, but visually icon-only. That is acceptable for a design gallery, weaker for a real product surface.

The right choice's green fill can imply "correct," "safe," or "selected" rather than simply "option B."
